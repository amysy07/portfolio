the challenge

Finding key elements that make a city unique

For this project, I was assigned to design a logo representing an international city, encapsulating its essence, artistic heritage, cultural richness, historical background, and modern-day vibrancy, without creating something cliche.

the solution

Drawing inspiration from traditional art mediums

My exploration of the city's art, history, and culture led me to Chiang Mai's rich artistic community and their beautiful and intricate wood carvings.

Research

Initially I dug into events that made the city unique, as well as their art and local mythology.

Â After identifying the categories I wanted to explore, I researched articles, books, and looked at images. Watching YouTube videos of Chiang Mai provided me with a first-person perspective of what it would be like to visit the city.

Key themes that I came across were: Chiang Mai's rich history in art; Naga, the mythological serpent river guardian; and their local lantern festivals.

Sketching

With the information I learned, I started sketching and experimented with positive and negative figures within the shapes I was building.

My first round of sketches weren't quite hitting the mark, and required me to dive back into deeper research and produce a new round of sketches with fresh ideas.

Conceptualization

I chose three concepts from my sketches that I felt strongly represented the brand and were unique. After sharing three different logos and receiving feedback, I selected one to enhance and elaborate upon.

Option #1

Falcon

During his youth, Choinard was part of a falconry club; this led to his love of the outdoors and rock climbing.

Option #2

Guanaco

The guanaco is a native animal of Patagonia, and a key symbol in its ecosystem. The textures of the fur are arranged to show the silhouette of the Fitz Roy mountains.

Option #3

Chock + Leaf

The chock is a nod to Choinard's love of rock climbing and the brand's origin. The lengua leaf, a native plant to Patagonia, makes up the negative space.

Iterations and
Final Logo

The final logo I chose was the guanaco, a relative of the alpaca that is commonly found in Patagonia.

Through my research I learned that they are native to Patagonia, and are an iconic symbol of the region known for their adaptability to harsh environments, which was also a fitting symbol and message that represented Patagonia, the company.

Hidden in the body of the guanaco is the silhouette of the iconic Fitz Roy mountain, which the founder of Patagonia attempted to ascend.
