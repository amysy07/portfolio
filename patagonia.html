the challenge

Paying tribute to the past while refreshing the present

Patagonia boasts a legacy spanning several decades. With a loyal customer base and a reputation for quality, my goal was to rejuvenate their brand image while honoring their esteemed history.

the solution

Hidden details that tell a deeper story

The primary objective was twofold: first, to conduct comprehensive research into the company's origins, evolution, and core values, and second, to craft a modernized logo that encapsulates its heritage while projecting a fresh identity.

Research

In crafting the new logo, I dug into the brand's past, studying its history and the founder's story.

This research helped me understand the brand's values and find inspiration for the design. I started by writing down words that came to mind when thinking of Patagonia, and looking for any connections.

I delved deep into the past of Patagonia's founder, Yvon Choinard, and all of his amazing feats and accomplishments. I also researched some of the native flora and fauna of Patagonia, trying to find a way to tie them to the brand in a symbolic way.

Sketching

With the information I learned, I started sketching, but found myself feeling "boxed-in". My first round of sketches weren't quite hitting the mark, and required me to dive back into deeper research and produce a new round of sketches with fresh ideas.

After hours of struggling to produce something I felt proud of, I finally had a moment of enlightenment. What if I tried playing with the negative shapes within the figures I was drawing?

Conceptualization

I chose three concepts from my sketches that I felt strongly represented the brand and were unique. After sharing three different logos and receiving feedback, I selected one to enhance and elaborate upon.

Option #1

Falcon

During his youth, Choinard was part of a falconry club; this led to his love of the outdoors and rock climbing.

Option #2

Guanaco

The guanaco is a native animal of Patagonia, and a key symbol in its ecosystem. The textures of the fur are arranged to show the silhouette of the Fitz Roy mountains.

Option #3

Chock + Leaf

The chock is a nod to Choinard's love of rock climbing and the brand's origin. The lenga leaf, a native plant to Patagonia, makes up the negative space.

Iterations and
Final Logo

The final logo I chose was the guanaco, a relative of the alpaca that is commonly found in Patagonia.

Known for their adaptability to harsh environments, I thought this was a fitting symbol and message that represented Patagonia, the company. The fur outlines the silhouette of the iconic Fitz Roy mountain, which the founder of Patagonia attempted to ascend.
